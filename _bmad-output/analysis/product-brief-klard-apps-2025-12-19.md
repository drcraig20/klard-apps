## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

The subscription economy has created a hidden "leakage economy" where users unknowingly lose hundreds of dollars annually. The problem manifests across five pain points:

1. **Privacy risk** (PRIMARY): Users feel unsafe giving credit card details to unknown vendors and trial services
2. **Lack of control**: No automated way to block renewals after a trial or one-time payment completes
3. **Visibility gap**: Subscriptions scattered across email receipts, bank statements, and memory
4. **Silent price increases**: Vendors raise prices without notification; users discover charges after the fact
5. **Cancellation friction**: Hard-to-find cancellation links designed to discourage users from leaving

**The #1 pain point is privacy.** Users want to try services but dread sharing their real payment information with every vendor. Current solutions force an impossible choice: bank access for convenience, or privacy at the cost of manual tracking.

### Problem Impact

- **41% of consumers** experience subscription fatigue
- **30% of annual subscriptions** are cancelled within the first month (buyer's remorse)
- **50%+ of trial starts** happen in the first 24 hoursâ€”users are impulse signing up without protection
- Average consumer spends **$133/month ($1,600/year)** on subscriptions, with significant hidden waste
- Data breaches have made users hyper-aware of payment data exposure

The psychological toll is real: users feel anxious about trials, paranoid about forgotten renewals, and powerless against "subscription creep."

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

| Solution | What They Do | Why They Fall Short |
|----------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Rocket Money / Truebill** | Subscription tracking + bill negotiation | **Requires full bank access**â€”privacy violation for many users |
| **Privacy.com** | Virtual cards for payment protection | **No subscription intelligence**â€”cards exist in isolation from tracking |
| **Bobby** | Privacy-first manual subscription tracking | **iOS-only, manual entry, no protection**â€”tracks but doesn't prevent |
| **Banks** | Transaction history | **No categorization, alerts, or protection**â€”purely reactive |

**The gap:** No solution combines subscription discovery + protection without requiring bank credentials. Users must choose between knowing their subscriptions OR protecting their payments. Klard is the first platform to bridge both worlds.

### Proposed Solution

**Klard bridges subscription intelligence and payment protection in a single platformâ€”without ever asking for bank credentials.**

**Core Value Loop:**
1. **Track**: Add subscriptions manually or via optional email scanning (read-once, privacy-first)

**How We Protect Your Data:**

## Credit Card Objection Handling

### The Classic Objection

> *"Why do I need Klard? I already have a credit card. I can pay at the end of the month and I get rewards."*

### Why Credit Cards Don't Solve This Problem

**What problem does a credit card actually solve for subscription management?**

- Does your credit card tell you Netflix raised prices by $3/month? **No.**
- Does it decline a charge when a free trial converts to $49.99? **No.**
- Does it show you all your subscriptions in one place? **No.**
- Does it give you a one-time card for that sketchy trial you want to try? **No.**

Credit cards are **approval machines**. They say YES to everything. Klard says YES when you want, and NO when you don't. That's the difference between a payment tool and a **control** tool.

### The Data Reality

| Metric | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| **Average subscription waste** | $252/year per person (C+R Research, 2023) |
| **Credit card rewards** | ~1-2% cashback = ~$20-40/year on subscriptions |
| **Net loss** | You're losing **$200+/year** even WITH rewards |

The "I get rewards" argument actually **reinforces** our value prop. You're earning $30 in rewards while losing $250 to forgotten subscriptions. That's not smart money managementâ€”that's a leaky bucket with a pretty bow.

### Systems Comparison

| Credit Card | Klard |
|-------------|-------|
| **Payment rail** (moves money) | **Control layer** (decides IF money moves) |
| Approves all authorized charges | Approves only what YOU want |
| Reactive (dispute after the fact) | Proactive (block before it happens) |
| No subscription intelligence | Full subscription visibility |

They're not competitorsâ€”they're **layers**. Klard sits between you and the merchant. Your credit card sits between Klard and the bank. We're the bouncer; they're the cashier.

### The Emotional Reality

> *"I signed up for a 7-day trial of some productivity app. Set a reminder to cancel. Forgot. Got charged $89.99 for an annual plan. Called my credit cardâ€”they said it was a valid charge because I agreed to the terms. Spent 45 minutes trying to get a refund from a company with no phone number."*

Credit cards don't protect you from **yourself**. Klard does. The "pay end of month" argument misses the point entirelyâ€”**the problem isn't WHEN you pay, it's WHETHER you wanted to pay at all.**

### Investor-Ready Answer

> **"Credit cards are designed to approve every charge. Klard is designed to approve only the charges you want. We're not replacing your credit cardâ€”we're adding an intelligence layer that prevents the $250/year in subscription waste that credit card rewards will never cover."**

**If they push on rewards, flip it:**

> **"Would you rather earn 2% back on charges you didn't want, or not pay for things you forgot to cancel? Klard saves users 10x more than credit card rewards ever could."**

### The "I'll Just Check My Statement" Objection

| Self-Management Claim | Reality |
|----------------------|---------|
| *"I'll review my statements"* | 85% of people don't audit subscriptions monthly |
| *"I'll set reminders"* | 62% of free trial users forget to cancel |
| *"I'll just dispute it"* | Average dispute takes 45+ minutes, often denied |

Klard automates what humans consistently fail to do. That's not a nice-to-haveâ€”that's a **behavior gap** we fill.

### Funding Architecture

**Model:** Option A â€” Pooled Klard Wallet with Internal Ledger

```
User pays via Stripe â†’ Klard credits internal ledger â†’ Cards draw from Klard's Airwallex wallet
```

| Component | Approach | Rationale |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Ledger** | Build your own (Java) | Provider-agnostic, full control |
| **User top-up** | Stripe payments | Familiar UX, fast integration |
| **Card funding** | Airwallex wallet | Single pool, Klard reconciles internally |
| **Authorization** | Remote Authorization webhook | Real-time approve/decline based on user balance |

**Ledger Tables:**
- `user_wallets` (user_id, balance, currency, updated_at)
- `transactions` (id, user_id, type, amount, reference, timestamp)
- `airwallex_reconciliation` (card_txn_id, user_id, amount)

**Provider Abstraction:**
- `CardProviderInterface` defines: createCard(), lockCard(), handleAuthorization()
- `AirwallexAdapter` implements for MVP
- Swap to Lithic/Stripe later without changing core logic
